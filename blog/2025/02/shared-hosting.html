Comments on Shared Hosting

2025-02-04

I've been recommending shared hosting for static sites for several years, and I think a few people have taken those recommendations.

The logic is that basically these commodity of PHP hosts have good hardware and networks for serving PHP, and serving static sites is easy as a result. And I also used Dreamhost for over 15 years -- since 2009. So I felt confident recommending it.

--

Well, Oils has moved from Dreamhost to Mythic Beasts, and I probably won't go out of my way to recommend it anymore.

I'm now us [Mythic Beasts][], which after 6 months of experimentation, seems to be excellent. And I hope I will stay on it for a long time.

But I think the general concept is.

I imagine:

  • Almost no customers use FastCGI. Mythic Beasts doesn't use FastCGI.
  • I'm sure most customers don't have cron jobs to sync access.log.*.gz and analyze them with Python and R. Most people are probably using web analytics
  • The SSH thing is less forgivable IMO. I think most people deploying PHP probably use SSH, or rsync over SSH, though I'm not entirely sure.

Conclusion

I just want to SSH to a Unix box :) I guess I am in the minority.

I am not going to recommend shared hosting in general anymore.

But since Iev only used them for 6 months, and not years, I am not going to make a loud recommendation :) Everything so far has been good though. Only issue is I had to work around access.log.
